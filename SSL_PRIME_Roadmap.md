# SSL PRIME Roadmap

## Phase 1: Pre-College Preparation (2026–2027)
### Goal:
Lay the foundation for dual enrollment at ACC and prepare for the TSI Assessment.

### Timeline:
- May 2026 to August 2027

### Key Steps:

1. **Texas Success Initiative (TSI) Assessment:**
   - **Goal:** Test college readiness (Reading, Writing, Math).
   - **Timeline:** Summer 2026
   - **Preparation:** Focus on Algebra, Geometry, Reading comprehension, and Essay writing. Take practice TSI tests weekly.
   - **Reason:** Required to qualify for ACC dual-credit courses.

2. **ACC Dual-Credit Enrollment (Fall 2027):**
   - **Duration:** 4 years (9th through 12th grade), concurrent with high school.
   - **Planned Courses:**
     - **Venice:** Physics I & II, Engineering Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Energy Systems.
     - **Eli:** Calculus I–III, General Physics, Astronomy, Modern Physics.
     - **Christian:** Programming (Python, C++), Data Structures, Electronics, Embedded Systems.

3. **Preparation for AFROTC:**
   - Begin physical fitness training (running, push-ups, sit-ups).
   - Develop leadership through team activities.

## Phase 2: College Roadmap (2028–2036)
### Dual Enrollment at ACC (2028–2032)

- **What You’ll Gain:** 
  - Mathematics, physics, and programming fundamentals.
  - College transcripts that strengthen your MIT application.

### Enter MIT (Fall 2032–Spring 2036)
- **Admission Requirements:** 
  - Exceptional academic achievement (GPA 4.0+), high SAT/ACT scores, recommendation letters, and a strong technical resume.

- **Key Details:**
  - **Degree Timeline:** 4 Years (Aerospace Engineering (Course 16) + quantum and software engineering compressed curriculum).
  - **Semesters:** ~16 weeks; ~5 classes per semester.
  - **Year 1:** Mechanics, Differential Equations, Intro to Aerospace.
  - **Year 2:** Plasma Physics, Quantum Mechanics, Embedded Computing.
  - **Year 3:** Propulsion Systems, Spacecraft Design.
  - **Year 4:** Capstone Projects (Interstellar Propulsion Systems, Astrophysics).

- **Benefits:** 
  - Access to cutting-edge labs, research facilities, and networking with industry leaders.

### Funding Plan:
  - **AFROTC Scholarship:** Covers tuition, books, and stipends.
  - **Supplemental Funding:** Research projects, industry partnerships, Air Force programs.

## Phase 3: AFROTC/Air Force Career (2036–2040)
### Joining AFROTC:
  - Apply during high school or early at ACC.
  - Maintain fitness and leadership scores.

### Active Duty:
  - **Duration:** 4 years minimum service commitment post-MIT.
  - **Base Assignments:**
    - Wright-Patterson AFB (Ohio): R&D in propulsion systems.
    - Edwards AFB (California): Aircraft testing and evaluation.
    - Kirkland AFB (New Mexico): Spacecraft operations.

### Benefits:
  - **Pay:** Starting salary ~$40,000–$60,000/year with increases for rank.
  - **Features:** Housing, healthcare, VA benefits, GI Bill for education.

### Funding SSL PRIME:
  - Save a portion of salaries (~$200,000 by 2040 as a team).
  - Apply for Air Force SBIR grants for research startups.
