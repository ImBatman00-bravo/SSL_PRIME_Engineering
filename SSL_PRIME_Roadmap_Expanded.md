# SSL PRIME Fully-Expanded Roadmap

## **Phase 1: Pre-College (Through 9th Grade)**
### Goal:
Prepare for dual enrollment at ACC and begin early foundations for AFROTC leadership and STEM skills.

### **1. Texas Success Initiative (TSI) Assessment:**
- **What It Is:** The TSI ensures students are ready for college-level coursework (Reading, Writing, Math).
- **Timeline:** Summer 2026
- **Preparation:**
  - Practice assessments online via the official TSI prep platform.
  - Key areas:
    - **Math:** Algebra and Geometry concepts, introducing college-level readiness standards.
    - **Reading/Writing:** In-depth reading comprehension and essay development.
  - Recommended Tools:
    - Khan Academy’s TSI Prep.
    - Use ACC Learning Labs for free tutoring opportunities.

## **Phase 2: ACC Dual-Enrollment (9th–12th Grade)**
### **Dual-Credit Courses Per Semester**:
For Venice, Eli, and Christian, the courses are selected specially to align with MIT’s academic expectations.

### **A. Year 1: Introduction to STEM Foundations**:
#### **Fall Semester**:
- **Mathematics for STEM:** College Algebra (3 credits).
  - Learn equation-solving, graphs, and modeling fundamentals.
- **General Physics I:** Mechanics, Waves, Heat (4 credits, lab component).
  - **ACC Physics Lab (Hands-On):**
    - Experiments with vectors, kinematics, forces.
    - Tools: Vernier LabQuest sensors.
- **Intro to Programming:** Python Basics (with projects).
  - **Learning Outcomes:** Build simple physics simulations.

#### **Spring Semester**:
- **Pre-Calculus (3 credits):** Trigonometry and advanced functions.
- **General Physics II:** Electricity and Magnetism (4 credits, lab).
- **Embedded Systems I:**
  - Christian’s hardware specialization begins, using Arduino Microcontroller boards.

---

### **B. Year 2–4 Highlights by Semester**:
- Advanced Research Concepts:
  - Thermodynamics (Venice).
  - Quantum Mechanics (Eli).
  - Data Structures/Algorithm Design (Christian).

_Key Resources_: Access to ACC Math Central tutoring for all ACC Mathematics labs, free software licenses like MATLAB for physics modeling.
